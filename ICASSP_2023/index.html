Modeling Irregular Voice in End-to-End Speech Synthesis via Speaker Adaptation

Authors: Ali Raheem Mandeel, Mohammed Salah Al-Radhi, Tamás Gábor Csapó

Email: {alimandeel, malradhi, csapot}@tmit.bme.hu

Abstract: End-to-end speech synthesis is frequently used in advanced text-to-speech (TTS) synthesis. Speech synthesizers may not create high-quality synthesized speech with limited target speakers' datasets. Additionally, because these models were developed using (irregular and regular voices), voices with frequent irregular phonation may not result in high-quality synthetic speech. In this paper, we adapted FastSpeech 2 with four target speakers with limited data of 100 sentences. In the objective evaluation test, the FastSpeech 2 produced speech more similar to the original speaker in terms of creakiness, i.e., successfully models irregular voices. Subjective tests reveal that more customized synthesized creaky voices are rated as less natural/similar than synthesized regular speech to the original target speakers' speech.

Datasets

  • Hi-Fi Multi-Speaker English TTS Dataset
  • 10 speakers: 4 Male + 6 Female
