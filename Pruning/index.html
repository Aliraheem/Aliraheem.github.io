Efficient Text-to-Speech Synthesis with Retraining-Free Pruning for Speaker Cloning

Authors: Ali Raheem Mandeel, Mohammed Salah Al-Radhi

Email: {alimandeel, malradhi}@tmit.bme.hu

Abstract

End-to-end text-to-speech (TTS) synthesis models produce highly natural speech, relying on large-scale pretraining with extensive datasets and adaptation for specific target speakers, such as voice cloning. However, as these models scale in size due to comprehensive pretraining, fine-tuning all parameters for individual speakers becomes computationally expensive and resource-intensive. In this paper, we propose applying Pruning by Weights and Activations (Wanda), a method initially developed for large language models, to reduce the parameter footprint of pretrained TTS models in voice cloning scenarios. Wanda leverages a simple yet effective metric: multiplying weight magnitudes by input activation norms to prune redundant parameters without requiring retraining, preserving speech quality while allowing efficient adaptation to cloned voices. We demonstrate how this approach yields lightweight TTS models suitable for deployment and addressing the scalability challenges of modern synthesis systems. These findings could help develop personalized text-to-speech applications, such as speech communication tools, to help people with speech difficulties.
